Fall 2020 Courses

  • MTH 94 - College Algebra

    • Course Objectives

    • Perform algebraic operations on polynomial, rational, radical, exponential, and logarithmic expressions.
    • Factor polynomial expressions completely.
    • Solve linear, quadratic, rational, exponential, and logarithmic equations using the graphical, algebraic, and numerical approaches.
    • Graph all basic functions including linear, absolute value, quadratic, cubic, rational, exponential, logarithmic, and piece-wise functions, and be able to determine their domains and ranges.
    • Differentiate functions from each other and demonstrate knowledge of the properties associated with each function.
    • Solve a system of equations with two unknowns.
    • Know when it is appropriate to use a graphing calculator to solve problems and/or verify solutions.

  • MTH 103 - Math for Elementary Teachers

    • Course Objectives

    • Categorize basic problem-solving strategies and utilize these strategies to solve application problems.
    • Apply the vocabulary of sets (and operations within sets) in communicating mathematical concepts.
    • Describe the development of the real numbers both historically and structurally.
    • Describe and correctly apply the arithmetic algorithms of real numbers.

  • MTH 303 - The Teaching of Mathematics in Middle Level and Secondary Schools

    • Course Objectives

    • to describe the processes involved in teaching mathematics (planning and preparation, classroom management, assessment, and the establishment of a positive learning environment)
    • to link appropriate learning theories to the teaching of mathematics
    • to identify and apply ancillary resources as instructional aides (in particular, information and communications technology - ICT)
    • to demonstrate mastery of content in the scope and sequence of middle school and secondary school mathematics
    • to explain the structure of state and national mathematics standards
    • to present lessons, labs and activities as practice teaching exercises (OPO) and perform local classroom visitations (40 hours practicum)
